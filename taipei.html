On Taipei, wear, & character

Taipei is like no other, an atypical capital amid other East-Asian cities of strass and glitter, where traditions and marks of history are relegated, confined to ever-shrinking areas, giving way to the new, the next-gen.

Singapore, Shanghai, and Tokyo are bright examples of flagship cities where beauty meets order. In some ways, Taipei does emulate these cities. Stroll down the Xinyi commercial district, toppled by the iconic 101, and you will find the tropes of East Asian flagship cities.

But Taipei does not shine where it tries to emulate its neighbors; it thrives where it embraces what came before.

Gritty. Gritty not only in character (take a look at the island's history) but in its physicality. Taipei possesses depth, created when matter mingles with time. This matrix results in layers of textures on top of one another, making Taipei a real-life collage, a mosaic of chaos.

We all fear time, but Taipei does not seem to. It reminds us of our ephemerality and offers brief glimpses of the artist who hides behind the passage of time. Brand new is not always better; signs of time possess a beauty no other entity can ever emulate.

And this is where the beauty of Taipei lays, in its wear, where time has kept some of its reigns, and where the mist of the new does not entirely take over.
