Bridging the cultural gap.

BINOME is a curative and cultural space that serves as a platform for Eastern artists looking to expand their reach to Western audiences.

BINOME was born from the realization that Eastern soft cultures have a more challenging time penetrating Western cultures than vice versa. It was the vision of two best friends looking to bridge their Eastern heritage with their Western upbringing. Having a hard time taking pride in their heritage or even being curious to learn more about it.

BINOME, the name, is taken from French, meaning a team of two, but also a mathematical equation composed of two unknown variables (the West and the East).

The notion of perspectives is at the core of BINOME’s identity. Inviting users to see a point-of-view outside of their own. To shift their paradigm, perceive a situation through a different lens. Ultimately, the platform’s identity vaguely mimics the role of art and artists it carries. The web experience recreates the effect of lens distortions, breaking free from the constraints of traditional flat screens and taking inspiration from the realm of virtual reality.
